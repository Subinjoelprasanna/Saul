Procedure - Missing Person / Kidnapping | File an FIR immediately at the nearest police station for a missing person. Police cannot insist on a waiting period. For minors, an FIR for kidnapping must be registered immediately. You can also contact the District Child Protection Unit or CHILDLINE 1098. | SaulGPT Legal Procedure Playbook | 2026-03-10
Section 359 IPC / Section 137 BNS | Kidnapping from India or from lawful guardianship - taking a person beyond or from their country or guardian without proper authority. | India Code - BNS 2023 / IPC 1860 | 2024-07-01
Section 363 IPC / Section 137 BNS | Punishment for kidnapping - imprisonment up to 7 years and fine. For kidnapping for ransom, punishment extends to life imprisonment. | India Code - BNS 2023 / IPC 1860 | 2024-07-01

# CHIT FUND AND INVESTMENT FRAUD
# ===========================
Procedure - Chit Fund Fraud | Chit fund companies must be registered under the Chit Funds Act 1982. If a company disappears with your money, file FIR under Section 420 IPC (cheating), file a complaint with state Registrar of Chit Funds, and approach Economic Offences Wing (EOW) of police. | SaulGPT Legal Procedure Playbook | 2026-03-10
Section 5 Chit Funds Act 1982 | Compulsory registration of chit funds and adherence to regulatory requirements. Unregistered chit funds are illegal and promoters can be prosecuted. | India Code - Chit Funds Act 1982 | 1982-08-19

# RTI (Right to Information)
# ===========================
Procedure - RTI Application | Any citizen can file an RTI application to any government/public authority by paying Rs 10 fee (by court fee stamp, DD or cash). Online RTI can be filed at rtionline.gov.in for central government departments. Information must be provided within 30 days. | SaulGPT Legal Procedure Playbook | 2026-03-10
Procedure - RTI First Appeal | If RTI response is unsatisfactory or not received within 30 days, a First Appeal can be filed with the First Appellate Authority within 30 days of deadline/response. A Second Appeal or complaint can be made to the Information Commission if still unsatisfied. | SaulGPT Legal Procedure Playbook | 2026-03-10

# PROPERTY REGISTRATION FRAUD
# ===========================
Procedure - Fraudulent Property Sale | If someone attempts to sell your property using forged documents, immediately file an FIR for forgery and cheating, apply for a stay on registration with the Sub-Registrar, and file a civil suit for declaration that the transaction is void. | SaulGPT Legal Procedure Playbook | 2026-03-10
Section 17 Registration Act 1908 | Documents relating to immovable property above Rs 100 must be compulsorily registered. Non-registered documents cannot be used as evidence for property transactions. | India Code - Registration Act 1908 | 1909-01-01
